Design Goals Updated: 1/7/1980

Immersive: First person point of view and full body control of action bring the player right into the action.

Realism: True representation of the navigated world and animal flight, hunting, and social behavior as well as endurance and health issues.

Aesthetically rich: While the world will be true to life in terms of physics, geography and weather, the aesthetics will be highly stylized with a basis in origami and heavily influenced by the art and culture of the areas through which a given player is navigating. For example when playing on the high rise ledges of New York City, the art work, music, will align with that environment vs. the steppes of Russia or the Mountains of Hokkaido.

Each player can customize their paper peregrine player with unique colors or logos.

The playing field is created around real peregrine roosting locations and migration routes.

Screen Shot

World creation is open sourced such that developers can work together to recreate virtual origami versions of real roosting locations and migration routes.

Players compete with one another for prime hunting roosts and prey to further their migrations to find the ancient aerie.

Health meters show the players’ level of injury and fatigue. If you are too weak or injured you cannot continue the migration until you regain strength through hunting, scavenging or using points acquired through passing stages. If friends are playing online they can share points if they are on the same board.

Characters Updated: 1/7/1980

Each player can be the main character as a peregrine falcon that they become during game play. The peregrine is made of paper in the game and can be painted with the colors and designs of the players choosing. Players can also be various prey animals from such as ducks, bats sandhill cranes, and ptarmigan.

It is estimated that worldwide there are as many as 2,000 unique bird species that the peregrine preys upon. Should a player opt to be a prey animal they would select 10 species to progress through. Should the player survive a breeding cycle as a prey species they would advance to the next species until completion of all 10 levels.
